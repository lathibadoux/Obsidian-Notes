* Who is the resume for?
	* It will first be reviewed by a technical recruiter - often someone without a technical background.
	* Make it easy for them to understand:
		* Focus on clear business impact
		* Highlight measureable impacts and value you added
	* Keep it simple
		* Lots of white space
		* One-column layout
		* Easy-to-read font
		* Simple one-line bullet points with action verbs and results

* Education
	* Highlight a strong GPA
	* Remove your graduation year to avoid age bias

* Technical Skills
	* Place it either at the top or the bottom
	* Avoid buzz words
	* Only include relevant skills

* Work Experience/Projects
	* Keep bullet points concise - ideally one line each
	* Use language from job descriptions
	* Your most recent experience should be the most relevant to the job description
	* You might have outgrown your title - your resume should reflect your impact
	* Show business value
		* Use metrics and outcomes to make your contributions clear
		* "Achieved [X] as measured by [Y] through [Z]" or "[Y]% improvement in [metric] by [Z]"

* Tips & Tricks
	* Use your LinkedIn to share more detail or context if needed.
	* Don't try to include everything - leave some things off to spark curiosity and fuel great interview conversations
	* Resume should show you don't just write code, but deliver impact through actions/technical skills
	* Send personalized DMs with job applications
	* Have someone proof read your resume to avoid typos

* Things to delete 
	* Action words like:
		* Help
		* Worked
		* Assisted
	* Graduation year
	* Hobbies

* Metrics?
	* Either *adding value* or *reducing waste*
	* "Reduced processing time by 4 hours" or "Increased processing efficiency to 4x"
